  • 1967
    The team was established in 1967 as a franchise of the NBA rival league, the American Basketball Association (ABA). They played in New Jersey as Americans from New Jersey during the first season, before moving to Long Island in 1968 and changing their name to the New York Nets.
  • 1974
    The Nets won two ABA titles, the first in 1974 and the other in 1976.
  • 2001
    One day after the recruitment, on June 28, 2001, Thorn exchanged Stephon Marbury and Johnny Newman with Phoenix Suns for star Jason Kidd and Chris Dudley (which the Nets later dismissed). The move has given the team something that it has lacked for most of its NBA existence, a leader who has improved his teammates. The Nets became one of the most exciting teams in the league in the 2001-2002 season. The team won their first Atlantic Division title, finishing with a record of 52-30 and being the first in the Eastern Conference.
  • 2019
    On June 30, 2019, the Nets hired Kyrie Irving and Kevin Durant for max contracts, in addition to signing DeAndre Jordan. Currently the team is in the so-called "Durant and Irving Era".
